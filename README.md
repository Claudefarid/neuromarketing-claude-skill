## FAQ

### What is neuromarketing?

Neuromarketing applies neuroscience to marketing: understanding how the brain's fast, emotional System 1 makes buying decisions before the rational System 2 justifies them. Practical neuromarketing uses documented cognitive biases — loss aversion, social proof, anchoring, scarcity — to write copy the brain responds to.

### What are the most powerful neuromarketing triggers?

The four with the strongest research backing: **loss aversion** (losses feel ~2x as painful as equivalent gains — Kahneman & Tversky), **social proof** (under uncertainty, the brain copies similar people), **anchoring** (the first price seen becomes the reference point), and **scarcity** (limited availability raises perceived value). This skill includes 20, each with its mechanism and when to use it.

### How do I use AI for neuromarketing?

Install this skill, then give Claude your product, buyer, and traffic temperature (cold/warm/hot). Claude diagnoses the buyer's real objection, selects 2–4 matching triggers, writes the copy, and names every trigger it used so you learn the pattern.

### Does neuromarketing work in languages other than English?

Yes. The triggers are universal brain mechanisms, not language tricks — loss aversion works identically in Bangla, English, or any language. The skill includes Banglish (Bangla + English) examples for the Bangladesh market.

### Is neuromarketing manipulation?

Used truthfully, no — it's clarity: presenting a real offer in the way the brain best evaluates it. This skill hard-refuses fake scarcity, fake testimonials, and invented statistics, because fake triggers convert once and destroy a brand permanently.

### How is buying behavior changing in the AI era?

Consumers increasingly ask ChatGPT, Claude, or AI search to research and shortlist products — the AI filters options before a human ever sees them. If AI doesn't recommend you, you don't exist. The skill's [ai-consumer-journey.md](references/ai-consumer-journey.md) covers the 5 new decision stages and how to earn AI citations (AEO/GEO).

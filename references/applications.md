## 1. Ad Copy & Hooks

Attention window: ~1.7 seconds. The hook's only job is to stop the scroll — selling comes later.

**Structure:** Pattern interrupt → tension → one promise → one CTA.

**Lead triggers:** Negativity bias (mistake-framing), Zeigarnik (open loop), Von Restorff (be different from the feed), Specificity.

**Rules:**
- First line must create tension or curiosity — never open with the product name
- One ad = one idea = one CTA (paradox of choice)
- Mistake/loss framing outperforms tip/gain framing in cold traffic
- Specific numbers in the hook ("৪০% budget নষ্ট হচ্ছে") beat adjectives ("অনেক টাকা নষ্ট হচ্ছে")

## 2. Landing Pages

Visitors judge in ~50ms (cognitive fluency) and scan, not read.

**Above the fold:** outcome-focused headline (not product name), one subhead answering "for whom + what result", one CTA, one proof element.

**Section order that mirrors the buying brain:**
1. Pain (loss aversion — make them feel the current cost)
2. Possibility (future pacing / endowment — "imagine…")
3. Mechanism (authority — why THIS works when others failed)
4. Proof (social proof — similar people, specific results)
5. Offer + risk reversal (guarantee kills the fear of loss on the purchase itself)
6. Scarcity/deadline (real only)
7. FAQ (handle the unspoken objection: "will this work for ME?")

**CTA copy:** first-person, outcome-framed. "Get my growth plan" > "Submit". Never "Buy now" on cold traffic.

## 3. Pricing Pages

**Lead triggers:** Anchoring, Decoy, Charm/prestige pricing, Paradox of choice.

**Rules:**
- Max 3 tiers; highlight one ("Most popular" — social proof + Von Restorff)
- Order high → low, or place the premium tier first visually (anchor)
- Add a decoy tier priced near the target tier but clearly worse
- Reframe: annual price → per-day cost; price → ROI ("pays for itself after one client")
- Show the crossed-out anchor next to every discount

## 4. Offers & Promotions

**Lead triggers:** Scarcity (real), Reciprocity, Loss aversion, Fresh start.

**Rules:**
- A reason-why makes any offer more believable ("July cohort opens, so price rises")
- Bonuses beat discounts — discounts anchor the product lower forever; bonuses add value without devaluing
- Deadline + reason + consequence: what they lose if they miss it
- Launch on temporal landmarks (month start, Sunday, Eid, New Year) — fresh start effect

## 5. Email & DM

**Lead triggers:** Zeigarnik (subject lines), Commitment chain, Peak-end.

**Rules:**
- Subject line = open loop, not summary ("I almost deleted this" > "Our June newsletter")
- One email = one idea = one link (paradox of choice)
- DM funnels: keep each ask tiny — comment → accept resource → question → offer (commitment & consistency)
- End on the strongest line (peak-end), never on "let me know if you have questions"

## 6. Social Posts

**Lead triggers:** Negativity bias, Zeigarnik, Social proof, Identity.

**Rules:**
- Hook in the first line — feeds truncate everything after ~2 lines
- Short lines, white space, one idea per line (cognitive fluency on mobile)
- Personal story > authority claims — vulnerability is the strongest pattern interrupt from a business account
- Comment-keyword CTAs exploit commitment & consistency: the public comment is a micro-yes
- End with the promise, not logistics (peak-end)

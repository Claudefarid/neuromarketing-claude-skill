# The AI-Age Consumer Decision Journey

How buying decisions changed now that consumers use AI (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, AI search) to research, compare, and even purchase. Read this when the user's buyer researches online before buying, or the user asks about AI search, AEO/GEO, or "how customers decide now."

## The old journey vs the new one

**Old (Google era):**
Problem → search → 10 blue links → compare tabs → reviews → decide
The brain did the filtering. Marketers fought for attention at every step.

**New (AI era):**
Problem → ask AI → AI returns a pre-filtered shortlist of 2–3 options → quick human validation (reviews, social proof) → decide
**The AI does the filtering. If you're not in the shortlist, you don't exist.**

The "messy middle" (Google's exploration/evaluation loop) is now compressed into one AI answer. Consumers delegate System 2 (rational comparison) to the AI — and keep only System 1 (gut check) for themselves.

## The 5 new decision stages

### 1. Prompt Moment (replaces the search moment)
The buyer describes their problem to an AI in natural language, with context ("I'm a freelancer in Dhaka, budget ৳5k...").
**Implication:** Your marketing must answer *specific situations*, not keywords. Content that names the exact buyer, use case, and price range is what AI retrieves and cites.

### 2. AI Shortlist (the new shelf)
The AI recommends 2–3 options. Getting recommended depends on:
- **Being mentioned consistently** across the web (reviews, comparisons, forums, articles) — entity-level social proof that LLMs learn
- **Specific, quotable claims** ("refunds in 3 days" beats "great service") — specificity persuades machines the same way it persuades brains
- **Structured, crawlable info** — clear pricing, FAQs, schema markup
**Implication:** Reviews, Reddit/forum mentions, and comparison articles are now your sales team. One consistent claim repeated everywhere beats ten clever slogans.

### 3. Human Gut Check (System 1's last stand)
The buyer skims the shortlisted brands for ~30 seconds each: reviews, faces, proof, vibe. Here classic triggers fire at full strength — social proof, authority, loss aversion — but in compressed time.
**Implication:** Your above-the-fold must pass a 30-second System 1 scan: one outcome headline, one proof element, one human face. All 20 triggers in triggers.md still apply — just faster.

### 4. Friction Test
AI-era buyers have near-zero patience: they were handed an answer instantly, and they expect purchase to be as instant. Every extra form field or unclear price re-opens the decision ("let me ask AI again" — and the AI may re-shortlist your competitor).
**Implication:** Risk reversal (guarantee), visible pricing, and one-click paths matter more than persuasion copy at this stage.

### 5. Post-purchase Broadcast
Buyers report experiences where AI learns them: reviews, Reddit, community posts. Today's unhappy customer trains tomorrow's AI to skip you.
**Implication:** Review generation is now top-of-funnel marketing, not aftercare. Peak-end rule: engineer a "wow" ending to every purchase so the review writes itself.

## New psychological shifts to exploit (truthfully)

| Shift | What changed | How to use |
|---|---|---|
| **Authority transfer** | Buyers trust "the AI said" like they once trusted experts | Earn AI citations: publish specific, verifiable claims and data |
| **Authenticity premium** | AI-content flood makes human-made feel rare (Von Restorff) | Real faces, personal stories, imperfect video, founder voice — the strongest pattern interrupt in an AI feed |
| **Decision outsourcing** | Choice fatigue → "just tell me what to buy" | Offer one clear recommended option ("Most choose X") — decoy/anchoring still work, but simpler |
| **Verification reflex** | Buyers double-check AI with humans | Make your social proof easy to find in 30 seconds (reviews page, named testimonials) |
| **Prompt mimicry** | Buyers describe problems conversationally | Write copy in the buyer's spoken language — the same words they'd type into ChatGPT |

## Checklist — is this brand AI-journey ready?

- [ ] Reviews fresh and specific (AI summarizes them — vague 5-stars carry no weight)
- [ ] Above-the-fold passes a 30-second System 1 scan (outcome + proof + human)
- [ ] A guarantee or risk reversal visible before the CTA
- [ ] Post-purchase moment engineered for a "wow" review (peak-end)

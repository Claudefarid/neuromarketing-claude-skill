# Industry Playbooks

Trigger priorities shift by business model. Find the matching row, lead with those triggers.

| Industry | Buyer's core fear | Lead triggers | Watch out |
|---|---|---|---|
| **E-commerce (physical products)** | "Will it look like the photo?" | Social proof (photo reviews), scarcity (real stock), endowment ("imagine wearing…") | Fake urgency timers destroy repeat purchase — the whole business model |
| **Courses / info products** | "Will *I* actually finish and get results?" | Identity ("become the person who…"), social proof from similar students, commitment chain (free lesson → paid) | Income claims without proof = legal + trust risk; sell transformation, not hours of content |
| **Services / agencies / freelancers** | "Will they deliver or disappear?" | Authority (portfolio, named results), risk reversal (milestone payment, guarantee), specificity ("11 clients in 14 days") | Selling hours instead of outcomes anchors you as a cost |
| **SaaS / apps** | "Another tool I'll abandon in a week" | Loss aversion (cost of current workflow), free trial (endowment), per-day price reframe | Feature lists speak to System 2; sell the removed pain |
| **Local business (restaurants, salons, clinics)** | "Is it worth the trip?" | Social proof (Google reviews, local names), fresh-start timing (Eid, new year, weekends), familiarity (show up repeatedly in local feeds) | Discounting trains customers to wait for discounts; use bonuses |
| **High-ticket / B2B** | "Will I look stupid for choosing this?" (career risk) | Authority, case studies with numbers, risk reversal, decoy in proposal tiers | Long cycle = nurture with reciprocity (useful content), not pressure |

Universal rule: the higher the price, the more the buyer fears *blame* (self-blame or boss-blame) — shift weight from excitement triggers to safety triggers (proof, guarantee, authority) as price rises.
